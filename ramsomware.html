Ransomware Awareness

Learn how ransomware attacks work and how to protect your data from these cyber threats.

What is Ransomware?

Ransomware is a type of malicious software (malware) that encrypts a victim's files and demands a ransom to restore access. It's often delivered via phishing emails, compromised websites, or malicious downloads.

How Does Ransomware Work?

The process of a ransomware attack typically follows these steps:

  1. The attacker sends a phishing email or malicious link.
  2. The victim clicks on the malicious link or attachment.
  3. The ransomware is downloaded and installed on the victim's device.
  4. The ransomware encrypts files, making them inaccessible to the user.
  5. The attacker demands a ransom, usually in cryptocurrency, in exchange for the decryption key.

  • Scareware: Displays fake security alerts to scare victims into paying a ransom.
  • Doxware: Threatens to release sensitive or private information unless the victim pays the ransom.

How to Spot a Ransomware Attack

  • Unusual file extensions or changes to file names (e.g., files changing to .encrypted).
  • Sudden and unexplained system slowdowns or crashes.
  • Ransom notes or demands for payment that appear on your screen.
  • Files that become unreadable or locked with encryption warnings.

What Should You Do if You Suspect Ransomware?

  • Disconnect your device from the internet to prevent further damage.
  • Run a full scan using antivirus software to detect and remove the ransomware.
  • Do not pay the ransom! Paying does not guarantee you will regain access to your files.
  • Restore your files from a backup if available.
  • Report the attack to the authorities or cybersecurity experts.

How to Protect Yourself from Ransomware

  • Backup your files regularly and store backups offline or in the cloud.
  • Use strong, unique passwords and enable two-factor authentication (2FA).
  • Keep your operating system and software updated to patch known vulnerabilities.
  • Be cautious when clicking on links or downloading attachments from unknown sources.
  • Install and maintain up-to-date antivirus software.
