Spyware Awareness

Learn how spyware works, how to recognize it, and how to protect yourself from it.

What is Spyware?

Spyware is a type of malicious software that secretly monitors and collects information about a user's activities without their consent. It can track browsing habits, capture personal information, and even control a user's device.

How Does Spyware Work?

Spyware typically follows these steps to infiltrate and monitor your device:

  1. The attacker tricks the victim into downloading and installing the spyware through a malicious website, email, or software bundle.
  2. Once installed, the spyware runs silently in the background, without the user's knowledge.
  3. The spyware gathers sensitive data such as login credentials, browsing history, and personal files.
  4. The attacker can then use or sell this data, or further exploit the system.

Common Types of Spyware

  • Adware: Displays unwanted ads to the user, often tracking their browsing habits to target ads.
  • Keyloggers: Records keystrokes to capture passwords, messages, or other sensitive information.
  • Trojans: Disguised as legitimate software, trojans install spyware or other malicious software on the victim's device.
  • Browser Hijackers: Alters the settings of your browser (e.g., homepage, search engine) to gather data or display ads.

How to Spot Spyware

  • Your computer or device runs slower than usual.
  • Unusual or unexpected pop-ups and ads appear on websites you visit.
  • Your browser settings (homepage, default search engine) change without your permission.
  • Unexplained activity such as new toolbars or icons on your device.
  • Your personal information, such as passwords or credit card details, is being used without your consent.

What Should You Do if You Suspect Spyware?

  • Run a full system scan with updated antivirus software to detect and remove the spyware.
  • Check for unusual programs in your installed applications and remove anything suspicious.
  • Reset your passwords for critical accounts like email, banking, and social media.
  • Disconnect from the internet to prevent further data transmission to the attacker.
  • Report the incident to the authorities or your cybersecurity service provider.

How to Protect Yourself from Spyware

  • Install reputable antivirus software and keep it up to date.
  • Be cautious when downloading files or software from untrusted sources.
  • Use an ad blocker to prevent unwanted pop-ups and malicious ads.
  • Regularly update your operating system and applications to close security vulnerabilities.
  • Use strong, unique passwords for your accounts and enable two-factor authentication (2FA).
