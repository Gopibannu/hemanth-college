Understanding Ransomware and Staying Safe

What is Ransomware?

Ransomware is a type of malware that encrypts your files, blocking access until a ransom is paid. This can lead to data loss and financial damage if not handled properly.

How Does Ransomware Infect Devices?

Ransomware can spread through:

  • Phishing emails with malicious attachments or links
  • Downloads from untrusted websites
  • Exploit kits on compromised websites
  • Network vulnerabilities

What to Do if Your Device is Infected with Ransomware?

If you suspect ransomware infection:

  • Disconnect from the internet to prevent data from being stolen or further spread.
  • Do not pay the ransom, as this does not guarantee data recovery.
  • Use antivirus software to attempt to remove the ransomware.
  • Restore your files from a backup if available.

How to Stay Safe from Ransomware?

To protect yourself from ransomware:

  • Use reputable antivirus software and keep it updated.
  • Back up important files regularly and store backups offline.
  • Be cautious with email attachments and links from unknown sources.
  • Keep your operating system and software up to date to patch vulnerabilities.
