DDoS Awareness

Learn how to understand and protect yourself from DDoS cyber threats.

What is DDoS?

A Distributed Denial of Service (DDoS) attack is a malicious attempt to disrupt the normal traffic of a targeted server, service, or network by overwhelming the target with a flood of internet traffic.

How Does DDoS Work?

DDoS attacks typically follow these steps:

  1. The attacker uses a network of compromised devices (a botnet) to initiate the attack.
  2. The botnet floods the target with massive amounts of traffic.
  3. The targetâ€™s server or network becomes overwhelmed and unable to respond to legitimate traffic.
  4. The attacker continues to flood the target with traffic, causing service outages or downtime.
  5. The attacker may demand ransom or disrupt business operations until the target complies with their demands.

Common DDoS Tactics

  • Volume-Based Attacks: Overloading the network with large amounts of traffic (e.g., UDP floods).
  • Protocol Attacks: Disrupting the communication protocols of the target (e.g., SYN flood).
  • Application Layer Attacks: Targeting specific application functions to exhaust server resources (e.g., HTTP floods).
  • Botnets: Networks of infected devices used to carry out DDoS attacks.

How to Spot a DDoS Attack

  • Significant slowdown or unresponsiveness of your website or server.
  • Unexpected spikes in traffic from unusual locations.
  • Complete outages or inability to access your online services.
  • Server logs showing high levels of traffic from a single or several IP addresses.

What Should You Do if You Suspect a DDoS Attack?

  • Monitor network traffic for abnormal spikes or unusual patterns.
  • Contact your hosting provider or network administrator to investigate.
  • Use DDoS mitigation services to filter out malicious traffic.
  • Activate rate-limiting measures or temporary blocking of suspicious IP addresses.

How to Protect Yourself from DDoS

  • Implement DDoS protection and mitigation tools, such as Web Application Firewalls (WAFs).
  • Distribute your network infrastructure across multiple data centers or use cloud services with built-in DDoS protection.
  • Use rate-limiting and geo-blocking techniques to limit traffic from specific regions.
  • Ensure redundancy and failover systems are in place to maintain service availability during an attack.
