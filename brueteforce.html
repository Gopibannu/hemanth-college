Brute Force Attack Awareness

Understand the dangers of brute force attacks and how to protect yourself.

What is a Brute Force Attack?

A brute force attack is a type of cyber attack where an attacker systematically attempts every possible password combination until they find the correct one. Itâ€™s a time-consuming process, but with enough resources, it can break into almost any account.

How Does a Brute Force Attack Work?

Brute force attacks typically follow these steps:

  1. The attacker obtains the username of a target account.
  2. The attacker uses automated software to try different password combinations.
  3. If the password is correct, the attacker gains access to the account.

Types of Brute Force Attacks

  • Dictionary Attack: Uses a list of commonly used passwords and variations.
  • Hybrid Attack: Combines dictionary attacks with variations (e.g., adding numbers or symbols).
  • Reverse Brute Force: Uses a common password across many usernames.

How to Spot a Brute Force Attack

  • Multiple failed login attempts within a short period of time.
  • Account lockouts after repeated incorrect password attempts.
  • Unusual login locations or IP addresses.

How to Protect Yourself from Brute Force Attacks

  • Use long, complex, and unique passwords for each account.
  • Enable two-factor authentication (2FA) to add an extra layer of security.
  • Limit login attempts to prevent multiple tries in a short period of time.
  • Monitor your accounts for suspicious activity and set up alerts.
