Phishing Awareness

Learn how to spot phishing attempts and protect yourself from cyber threats.

What is Phishing?

Phishing is a form of cyber attack where attackers impersonate legitimate organizations or individuals to steal sensitive information like passwords, credit card numbers, or login credentials.

How Does Phishing Work?

Phishing typically follows these steps:

  1. Attacker sends a fraudulent email or message.
  2. The victim clicks on a malicious link or opens an attachment.
  3. The link directs the victim to a fake website that looks legitimate.
  4. The victim enters personal information (e.g., password, bank info).
  5. The attacker collects the information for malicious use.

Common Phishing Tactics

  • Urgency: "Act Now! Your account will be locked!"
  • Impersonation: Emails from trusted services like banks or online stores.
  • Suspicious Links: Fake websites with URL differences like "paypa1.com" instead of "paypal.com".
  • Attachments: Malicious attachments disguised as invoices or receipts.

How to Spot a Phishing Attempt

  • Spelling or grammar mistakes in the message.
  • Suspicious or mismatched sender email addresses.
  • Generic greetings like “Dear Customer” or “Dear User”.
  • Unexpected attachments or links in emails.
  • Lack of personalized information.

What Should You Do if You Suspect Phishing?

  • Don’t click on links or download attachments.
  • Verify the sender’s email address by checking for discrepancies.
  • Contact the organization directly (via their official website or phone number).
  • Report phishing attempts to authorities or your email provider.

How to Protect Yourself from Phishing

  • Use strong, unique passwords for each account.
  • Enable two-factor authentication (2FA) where possible.
  • Use phishing detection tools in your email.
  • Stay updated on the latest phishing trends.
